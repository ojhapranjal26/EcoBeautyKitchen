Sugar + Honey Exfoliator for Glowing Skin

By Stephanie Pollard | Reviewed by Dr. Jennifer Haley, M.D., FAAD | August 4, 2024

Post may contain affiliate links.

Sugar in your diet won’t do you any favors, but sugar in your beauty products? Well, that’s a totally different story. Sugar is a naturally gentle abrasive, so it’s perfect for polishing sensitive areas like your face and lips. When your skin starts acting up, just whip up this simple sugar scrub for your face to buff away flakes, clear pores, and lock in moisture.

Homemade sugar + honey exfoliator, perfect for glowing skin.

When it comes to our bodies, we’re often told to avoid sugar, but incorporating it into your beauty routine can actually be beneficial! What better way to do that than with this sugar scrub for the face?

Benefits of Sugar for Skin

  • It draws in moisture: As a natural humectant, sugar helps lock in moisture so your skin stays hydrated and supple.
  • Gentle on sensitive skin: Fine-grain sugars, such as caster or brown sugar, gently exfoliate without irritating the skin.
  • Rich in glycolic acid: Sugar is a natural source of glycolic acid, which supports skin cell turnover and helps treat irritation and discoloration.

Honey, a key ingredient in the exfoliator, is known for its anti-bacterial properties.

How to Make a Sugar Scrub for the Face

This homemade sugar scrub is formulated for sensitive skin and is packed with raw honey to fight bacteria, bentonite clay to gently exfoliate, and vitamin E oil to nourish and heal.

Materials

  • 1 cup caster sugar (brown sugar can be used)
  • ¼ cup bentonite clay
  • 15 drops chamomile essential oil
  • ¼ cup grapeseed oil
  • 1 tablespoon raw honey
  • ½ teaspoon vitamin E oil

Gently massage the scrub in circular motions for a natural glow.

Instructions

Combine all ingredients in a bowl and mix well. If you'd like a looser scrub, add more oil. To use, apply a small amount to clean skin, massage gently for about 60 seconds, then rinse with warm water. Use twice a week for best results.

FAQs

Q: Is there an alternative to bentonite clay?

A: Yes! Any cosmetic clay will work, or you can leave it out entirely.

Q: My scrub is dry and crumbly. What should I do?

A: Just add more oil to loosen it up. If you're ready to use the scrub, add a bit of water to a small scoop and apply as usual.

Q: Are DIY scrubs safe?

A: Absolutely, as long as you use gentle ingredients like caster sugar and don't scrub too hard.
